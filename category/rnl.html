Reading

  • The Gervais Principle II by Venkatesh Rao.
    Want to say that the theory may not account for “lifestyle businesses”. Think sustainable software business. situations where the members at the top are the only members, people own roughly equal amount (no VC/”founder”-levels of equity) and no one’s creating less value than they’re getting (Losers). In this situation (lifestyle business/sustainable software business) it is possible for all members of the group to be “Sociopaths”. Rao separates “Loser” from the cultural context, curious if it’s apt to be consistent and do this for “Sociopath” category too.
    OR
    Is it because “lifestyle businesses” do NOT abide by the MacLeod Life Cycle (which he penned himself) - they do not live and grow and die/stagnate, they are in a perpetual “stagnation” (not in a pejorative sense) - that Gervais Principle is not meant to address these?

Day 1 of big post-grad Asia trip: 15-hour layover in LAX (planned purposefully, in case my AUS-LAX leg before Hong Kong got delayed, which it didn’t!)

On the plane

  • I’m only carrying the Daypack part of my Osprey Farpoint 55 and a carry on-sized piece of luggage.
  • Had an entire row to myself!
  • Delta’s in-flight entertainment screen, despite people owning stuff on their phones and laptops instead (I’m convinced other airlines have given up completely on this) is awesome. Tried to watch 12 Strong (which was good so far, but wasn’t really in the mood for it), but I saw the guy in the seat across from me watching Happy Death Day, which looked much funnier, realized I was too tired to watch anything anyway and fell asleep. Then I watched an episode of Bong Appétit from VICELAND, which is about infusing weed into foods as varied as Texas BBQ and sashimi. I don’t actually smoke or get high regularly, but it was pretty interesting.

Observations

  • Upon “hopping (sic) off the plane at LAX” - saw the guy who was across the aisle from me blast “Party in the U.S.A.” by Miley Cyrus and taking a video for his Snap story.

  • I had trouble finding reputable, reasonably-priced luggage storage for my one carry-on at first, and then I found Bagbnb, which is like Airbnb but for storing your luggage! There was a stopping point about a 15-min walk from Venice Beach, which is where I wanted to go.

  • Abbot Kinney Blvd is one of the most charming blocks I’ve ever walked down
  • When I was walking along the houses lining the beachfront, I overheard this British-sounding lady telling this older American man, “so that’s the point of the blockchain, which is what Bitcoin is based upon, …”. I wanted to keep listening, but didn’t want to interrupt two people.
  • Everyone here is dressed so well (that may be because I’m walking around Venice)! Felt like a tourist in my Asics and beige (non-cargo) shorts.
  • I’m currently writing this from Greenleaf Gourmet Chopshop in Venice. It’s fucking awesome, from the lemongrass chicken salad I got, to the music they’re playing (a lot of music I haven’t heard of before, and also Yelle and HAIM), and the general vibes of the place.
  • Intelligentsia Coffee in Venice

  • The Albright on the Santa Monica Pier

  • My Lyft driver from the Bagbnb stop to the LAX was a Lebanese man who had a lot to say about the economy and how things used to be in California. Turns out he is also familiar with the works Nassim Nicholas Taleb!

Random observations from my 15-hour layover in LAX today (I spent most of that time around Venice and Santa Monica):

  • I used this service called “Bagbnb” to reserve space for the small carry-on I brought with me. I only paid $6 for the whole day of storage, and the dropoff site was the office of a neat coworking space, which was walking distance from Venice Beach
  • Abbot Kinney Boulevard in Venice is one of the most charming shopping blocks I’ve ever been to
  • Bird (those electric scooters) is ubiquitous in Santa Monica (where the company is based out of)
  • My Lyft driver from my Bagbnb to LAX was a Lebanese man who had a lot of insights about the economy, plus he was familiar with the author of one of the books I’m currently reading (Taleb, who is also Lebanese).
